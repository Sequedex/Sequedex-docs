*************
Tree of Life
*************

In order to provide correspondence to NCBI's taxonomy and the reference genomes, this chapter presents the list of reference genomes linked back to NCBI on the basis of genus and species names.  The complete directory name from the NCBI ftp site also appears below, and the combination allows the current release of each genome to be located.  

Sequedex was distributed with a signature list and phylogenetic classification based on a bacterial one-per-genus tree of 403 taxa (bact403.1).  Since that time, we have increased both the breadth and resolution of this tree to include bacteria, archaea, and eukaryotes at the one-per-species resolution.  The data module for the larger tree will be released shortly, but the reference list below serves both trees.  Both trees were based on an amino acid alignment of the concatenated RNA polymerase beta and beta prime subunits (for bacteria) or the analogous subunits of RNA pol II (for eukaryotes).  

Even with a fixed tree topology, the phylogenetic profiles can be visualized in a variety of ways, and this chapter attempts to start the user with phylum-level, family-level, and species-level identifications of what is present in a sample.  Although labeled as phylum and family, we took great liberty in using the pair of classifications that best suited the density of reference genomes in each region of the phylogeny.  In any case, we provide a suggested division that is both spanning and non-overlapping.  By clicking on the taxon names, the user can easily compare to all levels of the taxonomy assigned by NCBI.  

As described in the Introduction, Sequedex captures the uncertainty inherent in classifying fragments of DNA by making phylogenetic assignments to the nodes of a binary phylogenetic tree, and not to individual taxa.  Consequently, some node numbers are indicative of phyla, some nodes are indicative of families, and some are specific to a pair of taxa.  In the table below, we provide all three types of information.  The columns labeled 'tol' and 'b403' list the node of the respective trees that are immediately above each of the taxa.  Rows left blank under 'b403' were not present in that database.  Furthermore, for the 'tol' tree, the nodes corresponding to the groups are also indicated.  Because of the way the nodes on the trees were numbered, through a depth-first traversal, node numbers intermediate between these two extremes are intermediate nodes between the group name (family or phylum) and the leaves.

There are undoubtably mistakes in this classification.  The nature of the signature peptide method, however, is such that these mistakes serve to blurr out phylogenetic signals, not result in spurious assignments to specific nodes.
